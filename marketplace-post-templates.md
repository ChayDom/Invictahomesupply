# Cross-posting templates

The goal of every Marketplace/group post is the same: give just enough to
stop the scroll, then send them to your site for the full photos, details,
and reserve button. Don't put your whole catalog description on Marketplace
— that gives people no reason to visit invictahomesupply.com.

Replace the bracketed parts before posting.

---

## Facebook Marketplace — single item listing

**Title:** [Item name] — [Condition] — $[Price] (was $[Was Price])

**Description:**
```
[One line on what it is / why it's a good deal]

More photos + full details: invictahomesupply.com/shop.html
Text or call [Phone] to reserve — no online payment needed, pay when you pick up.

New inventory drops weekly — text START to [Phone] to get notified first.
```

**Example:**
```
Waterproof oak vinyl plank flooring, brand new box, 20mil wear layer.
Priced way below retail — grabbed a pallet, passing on the savings.

More photos + full details: invictahomesupply.com/shop.html
Text or call (555) 123-4567 to reserve — no online payment needed, pay when
you pick up.

New inventory drops weekly — text START to (555) 123-4567 to get notified first.
```

## Local Facebook groups (buy/sell/trade, home reno, DIY)

Keep this shorter and more conversational than a Marketplace listing —
groups respond better to a plain post than an ad.

```
Got a fresh batch of [category, e.g. "luxury vinyl flooring and a few brand
new appliances"] in this week — all brand new, priced fair, local pickup only.

Full inventory with photos: invictahomesupply.com

Text/call [Phone] to reserve anything, no online payment needed.
```

Post this once a week, ideally right after you update your "New This Week"
section, so the group post and the site match.


---

## Tips

- Always link to `invictahomesupply.com/shop.html` (not just the homepage)
  when promoting specific items — it drops people straight into the catalog.
- Post consistently (same day each week) so regulars know when to check
  both the group and the site.
- Once you have real photos, lead posts with your *best* photo — it's the
  first thing people see in feed before they click anything.
